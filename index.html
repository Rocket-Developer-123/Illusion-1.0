Motion

Counter-Rotating Wheels

Adjacent wheels rotating in opposite directions create a sense of impossible interlocking motion — your brain tries to find the "teeth" that must connect them.

Geometry

Café Wall Illusion

The horizontal mortar lines appear to slope — but they are perfectly parallel. The offset rows cause each "tile" to tug your perception of the dividers.

Contrast

Scintillating Grid

Dark phantom dots appear and vanish at the intersections as your gaze moves across the grid — but the dots are never actually there.

Depth

Ambiguous Cube

A wireframe cube rotates endlessly. Without shading, your brain oscillates between two equally plausible 3-D interpretations — stare until it flips.

Aftereffect

Motion Aftereffect

Stare at the centre dot for 20–30 seconds, then look at the back of your hand. The skin will appear to breathe — a classic motion aftereffect caused by fatigued direction-sensitive neurons.
